# Use Case Inspiration

Stuck on what to build? Here are broad themes and ideas that apply to almost any kind of work. Pick one, combine two, or use these to spark your own idea.

The best apps solve a real, specific problem you've actually experienced, not a theoretical one.

With around 24 hours to build, you can aim for a real multi-feature tool, not just one tiny thing. The trick is to pick an idea with a clear core you can get working first, then layers you can add on top once it holds together.

This kit ships with a sample professional-services dataset in `mockdata/` (a fictional firm) that you can build against if you don't have data of your own to hand. You can equally bring your own domain and make something for the work you actually do.

---

## Your Day-to-Day Work

What do you actually spend your time on? Can any of it be automated or augmented?

- A **daily brief** that summarises what's on your plate and puts it in priority order
- A **tracker** for the things you have to keep an eye on: tasks, requests, approvals, anything with a status
- An **onboarding guide** for a new role that explains what the job involves and what to do first

---

## Working with Documents and Data

The repetitive, process-heavy work that takes time but not much judgement.

- **Summarise a long document** into a plain-English overview of what it says and why it matters
- **Extract key points** from a report, a form, or a set of notes and lay them out clearly
- **Compare two versions** of something and highlight what changed between them
- **Turn a messy export** (a clunky spreadsheet or data dump) into a clean, readable view

---

## Learning and Onboarding

Tools that help people get up to speed faster or more independently.

- A **quiz or explainer** that tests and builds knowledge on a specific topic
- A **plain-English explainer** that takes a dense piece of text and makes it understandable
- A **"first day" guide** that explains a process, the key people, and what a newcomer's role is
- A **how-it-works companion** that explains why something is done a certain way and what it protects against

---

## Relationships and Stakeholders

Tools that help you stay close to the people who matter and spot opportunities.

- A **contact or relationship tracker**: who knows who, when they last spoke, whether the relationship is warm or cold
- An **opportunity spotter** that surfaces signals worth acting on from public or internal sources
- An **events matcher** that takes someone's interests and suggests relevant things to send or invite them to
- A **prep brief** that pulls together what you already know about a person or organisation before a meeting

---

## Knowledge and Search

Tools that make the knowledge around you easier to find and use.

- A tool that makes **internal knowledge easier to find** instead of buried in folders and inboxes
- A **Q&A over a set of documents**: ask a plain-English question, get an answer grounded in your own material
- A **standard checker**: given an input, is this normal, an outlier, or something to flag?
- A **quick-reference primer** on a topic that people keep having to look up

---

## If You're Still Stuck

Ask yourself: *"What's the most annoying thing I do repeatedly that a computer should be doing?"*

That's usually the best app idea in the room.
